# Cyber Security GRC Case Study: Risk Assessment, IAM Review & Audit – CareConnect Ltd

## Overview

This project presents a practical Cyber Security Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) case study based on a fictional UK-based healthcare organisation, CareConnect Ltd.

The objective of this case study is to demonstrate a structured approach to identifying cyber security risks, evaluating control areas, and applying fundamental GRC principles in a real-world scenario.

---

## Key Areas Covered

- Risk Assessment (CIA Triad-based)
- Risk Register Development
- Asset Management (CMDB concept)
- Identity and Access Management (IAM) Review
- Audit Approach (Three Lines of Defence Model)
- Security Recommendations

---

## Company Context

CareConnect Ltd is a fictional healthcare provider inspired by NHS and private care environments. The organisation handles sensitive data including:

- Patient health records
- Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
- Staff data
- Internal communications

Due to the nature of its operations, strong security controls and compliance measures are essential.

---

## Risk Assessment

Risks were identified based on their impact on:

- Confidentiality  
- Integrity  
- Availability  

### Key Risks Identified:

- Unauthorised access to patient data  
- Phishing attacks targeting staff  
- Data loss due to inadequate backup processes  

---

## Risk Register

A structured risk register was developed to:

- Assess likelihood and impact  
- Assign risk levels  
- Define appropriate controls  
- Identify ownership  

Controls include:

- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Security awareness training
- Backup and recovery processes

---

## Asset Management

Asset visibility was analysed using the concept of a Configuration Management Database (CMDB).

Key assets identified include:

- Patient record systems  
- Staff laptops  
- Email systems  

---

## IAM Review

Access controls were reviewed to ensure only authorised users can access sensitive systems.

Key areas covered:

- Authentication mechanisms  
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)  
- Privileged Access Management (PAM)  
- User lifecycle management  
- Access reviews  

---

## Audit Approach

The audit approach follows the **Three Lines of Defence Model**:

1. Operational Management (First Line)
2. Risk & Compliance Functions (Second Line)
3. Internal Audit (Third Line)

Focus areas include:

- Control design  
- Control effectiveness  
- Identification of control gaps  

---

## Recommendations

- Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)  
- Enhance staff security awareness training  
- Maintain an accurate asset inventory  
- Conduct regular access reviews  

---

## Conclusion

This case study demonstrates how core GRC principles can be applied to identify risks, evaluate controls, and improve organisational security posture in a healthcare environment.
